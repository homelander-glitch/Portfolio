A bit about me

Who I am

I'm Aakash, a CSE student who got into frontend but kept getting pulled toward the lower-level stuff — how the network request actually works, what the camera API gives you, why audio playback behaves differently on mobile. My projects usually start with a problem I want to solve or a thing I want to understand, not "let me make something that looks nice." The design comes after I've figured out the logic. That order matters to me.

What I actually care about

Honestly? Whether the thing works. Does the speed test give accurate numbers, does the chess engine catch edge cases, does it run fine on a mid-range phone. I'm not anti-design — I do spend time on layout and feel — but I'd rather have a solid tool with decent UI than a beautiful thing that doesn't really do anything.

What I end up building

  • Tools that actually measure or test something — network speed, speaker health, camera info.
  • Stuff that talks to browser hardware APIs so it feels less like a website.
  • Logic-heavy things like chess rules, state machines, async flows — then a clean UI on top.
  • Things that don't fall apart on a phone.

Day to day

Second year B.Tech CSE. I do DSA, OS, and networks in class and find that a lot of it shows up directly in what I build on the side. I'm not trying to collect frameworks — I want to ship things that someone could actually open and use. A diagnostic tool for their phone, a chess game that plays by real rules. That's the goal.

Mobile Hardware Diagnostic Suite

Diagnostics

Built this to actually test what your phone can do. Checks download and upload speed, runs a speaker cleaner using low-frequency sound for 30 seconds, and checks your front and back cameras. Works way better on a phone than a desktop — that was the whole point.

  • Async speed test — download, then upload
  • LFO speaker cleaner (sounds weird, works)
  • Camera switch + basic sensor info

Chess (browser)

Game

Full chess in the browser, no frameworks, no libraries. Just vanilla JS and a grid. All the actual rules — legal moves, captures, check, checkmate. Took a while to get the edge cases right but that was kind of the fun part.
